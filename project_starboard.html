The game

Starboard is a 4 vs 4 multiplayer action game. You can select one of four characters with unique aestethics. You and your team-mates must cooperate to work several stations on a flying ship and work together to defeat the other team. Pick your role, will you control the engine, steer the ship, or operate the weaponry? You can communicate with your friends by using the in-game voice lines or text chat.

Score points by destroying the enemy ship or by capturing domination points. Fix the ship and its stations when they get damaged by picking up a repair kit and go to the broken component. Complete a task for the repair to complete. Reload the weapons by picking up an ammo kit and going to the empty weapon.

Download game here

The game can be started and explored as a single player, but all features are hard to explore solo.

The work

Ten person group project developed in Unity over the course of three months using the agile work method Scrum. At the beginning we all worked together on the original ideation and design of the game and began working more within our set roles as the project progressed. My role was general programmer and VFX artist and ended up being mainly responsible for:
• Coding the collision detection/resolution and respawn system of the ships.
• Coding the players pickup system of Repair- and Ammo-kits and their functionality.
• Creating all the VFX (basically 4) and coding their functionality, where some can be seen on my Artstation.
